About Light Pollution

Light pollution is a growing problem in urban areas. It not only obscures our view of stars but also has significant impacts on wildlife and human health. The introduction of artificial lights has transformed our nights, disrupting natural cycles.

What is Light Pollution?


Types of Light Pollution

Light pollution isn't just one thing - it comes in a few different forms, each with its own problems:

  • Skyglow: That hazy glow over cities that blocks out the stars. It's caused by light scattering in the atmosphere.
  • Glare: Super bright lights that hurt your eyes and make it hard to see. Think of unshielded streetlights or those blinding headlights.
  • Light trespass: When light spills over where it's not wanted, like into your neighbor's yard or bedroom window.
  • Clutter: A jumble of bright lights that's confusing and distracting. Common in cities with tons of signs and lights.

Sources of Light Pollution

Where does all this extra light come from?

  • Cities and towns: Streetlights, buildings, and those bright billboards all add to the problem.
  • Industrial areas: Factories and warehouses often have powerful lights that shine into the night sky.
  • Homes: Even our porch lights, garden lights, and security systems can contribute if they're not designed carefully.

Impact on Environment

Light pollution messes with nature's rhythm. Animals that rely on darkness get confused, and it can disrupt their feeding, mating, and migration.

Impact on Human Health

Too much light at night messes with our sleep. It disrupts our natural sleep-wake cycle and can even lead to health problems like insomnia, fatigue, and even more serious stuff.
