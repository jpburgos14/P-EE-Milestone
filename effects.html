Effects on Wildlife

Animals that are active at night rely on darkness to survive. Light pollution messes with their natural rhythms and can cause:

  • Disrupted ecosystems: Animals get confused about when to eat, sleep, and mate.
    • All that extra light wastes energy and contributes to climate change.
  • Loss of Night Sky: We're losin' our connection to the stars, and future generations might never experience the awe of a truly dark sky.
  • Impact on Astronomy: Light pollution makes it harder for astronomers to study the universe, hindering scientific discoveries.

Want to learn more about how light pollution affects wildlife? Check out this article from the International Dark-Sky Association: Light Pollution and Wildlife (IDA).
